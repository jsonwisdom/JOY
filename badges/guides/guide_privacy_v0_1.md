## Purpose

This guide explains privacy in JOYSPACE.

It helps Joy Gardeners decide what stays private, what may be family-only, and what can be shared safely.

Privacy is not a punishment.

Privacy is protection.

## Core Rule

```text
Private proof counts.
Family-only proof counts.
Shareable proof requires consent.
Authority remains false.
```

## Privacy Levels

### Private

Use private when something should stay with you.

Examples:

- personal reflection
- private screenshot
- sensitive learning note
- unfinished idea
- anything that feels too personal

Private proof is valid.

Private proof does not need to become public.

### Family-Only

Use family-only when something can be shared with trusted family or close circle, but not publicly.

Examples:

- family memory
- family encouragement
- family learning note
- private celebration

Family-only proof is valid.

Family-only proof does not automatically move into public spaces.

### Shareable

Use shareable when something is safe to show outside the family or private circle.

Examples:

- public README
- safe badge award note
- public drawing
- non-sensitive project commit
- general learning reflection

Shareable proof requires consent.

### Ask First

Use ask-first when you are unsure.

Ask-first means:

```text
Pause before saving, mirroring, posting, or sharing.
```

## What Not To Share

Do not share:

- passwords
- private messages without consent
- exact location
- school name
- schedule
- personal identifiers
- sensitive screenshots
- private family details
- identifiable minors without clear consent and safety review

## Witness Privacy

Witnesses confirm existence.

Witnesses do not need to see private details if a safer confirmation is enough.

A witness must not pressure someone to make private proof public.

A witness must respect the badge earner's privacy choice.

## Badge Privacy

A badge may be earned with private proof.

A badge may be celebrated privately.

A public badge display is optional.

Belonging does not require public exposure.

## Quick Decision Check

Before sharing, ask:

```text
Could this reveal something private?
Could this make someone uncomfortable?
Could this expose location, identity, school, or schedule?
Did everyone involved consent?
Would private be safer?
```

If unsure:

```text
Keep it private or mark ask-first.
```
