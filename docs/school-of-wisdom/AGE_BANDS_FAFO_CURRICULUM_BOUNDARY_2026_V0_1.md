## Purpose

This document defines a safe age-band boundary for extending the School of Wisdom beyond ages 7–14.

The goal is not to encourage risky behavior.

The goal is to teach pattern recognition, consequence literacy, consent literacy, reputation literacy, and evidence-safe decision-making.

---

## Existing Band

### Ages 7–14 — Daily Review of the Chaos

Focus:
- school-safe humor
- family arguments
- homework receipts
- chores
- screenshots
- green checkmarks
- simple substitution errors

Core question:

> Did we do the thing, or did we only say the thing?

---

## New Band

### Ages 15–17 — Matrix Introduction

**Working title:** Intro to the Matrix: Choices, Screens, Status, Consequences

Topics may include:
- parties
- social pressure
- dating and attraction
- clout
- online reputation
- substances as risk surfaces
- screenshots and receipts
- consent and boundaries
- promises versus proof
- performance versus reality

Boundary:

This band does not teach minors how to use alcohol, cannabis, or sex as gameplay.

It teaches minors how to recognize pressure, risk, manipulation, missing receipts, and false green signals.

Core substitutions:

```text
Attention ≠ love
Clout ≠ reputation
Invite ≠ safety
Consent theater ≠ consent
Being present ≠ being ready
Saying yes once ≠ saying yes forever
Substance available ≠ good decision
Screenshot posted ≠ truth verified
```

Core question:

> Is this choice real, or is the room trying to choose for me?

---

## Adult Band

### Age 18 — FAFO Training

Focus:
- adult consequences
- contracts
- employment
- leases
- dating responsibility
- money mistakes
- public behavior
- criminal/civil boundaries
- online permanence
- screenshots before escalation

FAFO means:

> Choices have consequences. Learn the boundary before the boundary teaches you.

Core substitutions:

```text
Legal age ≠ wisdom
Freedom ≠ immunity
Confidence ≠ competence
Public post ≠ private joke
Opportunity ≠ obligation
```

---

## Above 18 — FAFO Applied

Focus:
- adult accountability
- reputation systems
- work, money, family, law, health, and public trust
- evidence-safe conflict
- repair after mistakes
- knowing when not to play

Core question:

> What did you choose, what happened, what receipt proves it, and what repair is possible?

---

## AI Systems Stable / What Is Not?

AI systems may help stabilize:
- templates
- reminders
- logs
- review questions
- pattern detection
- neutral phrasing
- evidence organization

AI systems do not stabilize:
- human desire
- intoxication
- peer pressure
- consent
- legal risk
- reputation collapse
- violence
- addiction
- loneliness
- shame
- panic

AI can hold a flashlight.

AI cannot walk your feet.

---

## Safety Rules

1. No instruction for minors to obtain or use substances.
2. No sexual coaching for minors.
3. No targeting real people.
4. No humiliation as curriculum.
5. No fake authority.
6. No fake green.
7. If harm risk appears, pause the game and seek trusted human support.
