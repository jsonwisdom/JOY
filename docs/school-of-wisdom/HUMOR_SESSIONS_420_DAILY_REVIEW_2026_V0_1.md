## Purpose

Humor Sessions 4:20 is a daily family-safe review ritual for spotting substitution errors in ordinary chaos.

The session is not about proving accusations.

It is about teaching kids, parents, and teachers to ask:

1. What happened?
2. What did not happen?
3. Did someone treat them as the same thing?
4. What receipt would prove the difference?

---

## Core Rule

> Humor is signal, not evidence.  
> Culture is playable, but people are not targets.  
> No receipt, no authority.  
> 先存证，再吵架。

---

## Daily Format

### 4:20 Session Flow

1. **Cold Open** — one funny real-world confusion, school-safe.
2. **Spot the Substitution** — identify X happened / Y did not happen.
3. **Child Audit** — explain the missing step as if to a six-year-old.
4. **Receipt Check** — what would prove the claim?
5. **Laugh Without Targeting** — joke about the mechanism, not the person.
6. **Documentary Note** — record the lesson as observed-only.

---

## Documentary Frame

This is a documentary of pattern recognition, not a court, not a prosecution, and not a claim of authority.

The camera watches the chaos.
The child asks the clean question.
The green checkmark stays sad until verified.

---

## Age Band

### Ages 7–10

Focus:
- simple examples
- school and family life
- chores, homework, screenshots, missing promises

Question:
> Did we do the thing, or did we only say the thing?

### Ages 11–14

Focus:
- group projects
- online platforms
- dashboards
- rules versus reality
- screenshots and receipts

Question:
> What evidence would make this clear without yelling?
