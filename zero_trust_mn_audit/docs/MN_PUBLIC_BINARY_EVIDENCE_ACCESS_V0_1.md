# MN Public Binary Evidence Access v0.1

Courts are administrators of evidence, not owners of truth.

The public may inspect replayable proof surfaces through lawful public records, redacted records, hashes, metadata, receipts, and reproducible summaries.

## Binary Rule

We are made of binary in the audit sense: claims become public through data, files, hashes, logs, records, timestamps, and receipts.

## Court Role

Courts may administer, file, redact, seal, schedule, and index evidence.

They may not become the source of truth merely by controlling the doorway to the file.

## Public Role

The public may observe the lawful proof surface.

If raw records contain private or restricted information, the public surface becomes:

- redacted file
- hash
- metadata summary
- receipt
- replay script
- docket reference
- public index entry

## Crime Not Time

Observe the alleged action first.

Then weigh it against the court timeline.

Time is a measurement layer, not the crime itself.
