## Purpose

Klick Krazy Kid Edition explains the Minnesota school payment infrastructure audit in plain language.

It is built for one simple rule:

```text
Kids should get lunch and learning. Adults should prove the tech is safe.
```

## Kid Version

When a family pays for lunch or school activities online, the money may travel through a payment website, a card machine, a bank, a processor, and other vendors.

That does not automatically mean something bad happened.

It means adults should be able to answer:

```text
Who touched the payment?
Where did the data go?
Who got paid?
Was there a fee?
Was there a no-fee option?
Was the student protected?
```

## JAYWISDOM Gate

The JAYWISDOM gate means:

```text
No green light for systems touching children without receipts.
```

It does not mean:

- kids must pay for protection;
- schools are accused;
- vendors are guilty;
- foreign control is proven;
- fraud is proven;
- meals should be disrupted.

## Adult Records Request

Ask the district for:

1. school payment vendor contract;
2. payment processor, gateway, bank, and acquirer names;
3. fee schedule and no-fee payment option policy;
4. cafeteria POS or online portal vendor agreement;
5. PCI/security attestation;
6. payment data-flow description;
7. subcontractor and ownership disclosures;
8. student privacy and meal-access policies.
